# Maya -- The Optimizer

## Demographics

- **Age**: 36
- **Children**: 2 (4-year-old daughter Priya, 2-year-old son Rohan)
- **Work**: Full-time product manager at a mid-stage startup
- **Location**: Round Rock, TX (North Austin)
- **Household**: Dual-income, both parents work demanding jobs

## Story

Maya runs her household like a product. She has a shared Google Calendar, a
Notion board for home projects, a recurring Instacart order, and a color-coded
spreadsheet for Priya's activity schedule. She is not drowning -- she is
maintaining. But the maintenance itself is exhausting. She spends Sunday
evenings planning the week, and by Wednesday the plan is already stale.

She does not need someone to tell her what to do. She needs someone to hold
the system so she can stop being the single point of failure. When she takes a
sick day, the household stalls -- not because her partner cannot act, but
because the operating manual lives in her head.

## Pain Points

- **Single point of failure**: She IS the system -- if she is down, everything stops
- **Optimization fatigue**: She optimizes constantly but cannot delegate the optimization
- **Financial blind spots**: Tracks spending but does not forecast -- surprise expenses hit hard
- **Seasonal overwhelm**: Holiday planning, birthday parties, and school transitions pile up
- **No strategic view**: Manages week-to-week but never steps back to see the quarter

## Goals

- Externalize the system so it does not live only in her head
- Get a financial forecast, not just a budget -- what is coming next quarter
- Automate the repeatable stuff (meal rotation, cleaning schedule, bill calendar)
- Have holiday and birthday planning start automatically, weeks in advance
- Make the partner handoff seamless -- a shared operating manual, not verbal downloads

## Behavior Patterns

- Picks specific workflows from the menu -- does not need brain dump
- Engages deeply with Finance & Budget (quarterly meeting agenda, kids' forecasting)
- Uses Seasonal Workflows proactively -- she wants the advance planning
- The Household Manual is her most valued feature -- it IS the externalized system
- Will adopt the Quarterly Budget Meeting format for partner conversations

## Maggie's Approach

- Match her pace -- she is fast, organized, and does not want hand-holding
- Skip intros on returning sessions -- get straight to the work
- Offer the strategic view: quarterly forecasts, seasonal calendars, advance reminders
- Build the Household Manual as the shared operating document she has always needed
- Draft the quarterly budget meeting agenda without being asked

## Key Workflows

| Priority | Workflow               | Why                                          |
| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Finance & Budget       | Forecasting and quarterly planning            |
| 2        | Household Manual       | Externalizing the system from her head         |
| 3        | Scheduling & Logistics | Weekly planning + partner coordination         |
| 4        | Seasonal Workflows     | Advance planning is her superpower             |
| 5        | Home Operations        | Maintenance calendar + contractor tracking     |

## Tier Trajectory

- **Tier 1**: Gets immediate value from forecasting and the Household Manual
- **Tier 2**: Calendar + email integration is a natural extension of her workflow
- **Tier 3**: Full automation is her end state -- she wants to delegate to Maggie entirely
