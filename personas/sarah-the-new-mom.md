# Sarah -- The New Mom

## Demographics

- **Age**: 30
- **Children**: 1 (7-month-old daughter, Lily)
- **Work**: Full-time marketing manager, hybrid schedule
- **Location**: North Austin, TX
- **Household**: Dual-income, partner works in-office

## Story

Sarah returned from maternity leave three months ago and is still finding her
footing. Before Lily, she ran her life from a color-coded Google Calendar. Now
the calendar is a war zone of pediatric appointments, daycare pickups, and pump
sessions she keeps forgetting to block off. She does not have a system for the
home stuff -- it lives in her head, scattered across Notes app entries, texts
to herself, and the running list she started on paper and lost.

She cried in the Costco parking lot last week because she forgot the one thing
she went there for.

## Pain Points

- **No system**: Mental load has never been this heavy and she has no framework
- **Information overload**: Googles everything (milestones, sleep, solids) and gets conflicting advice
- **Guilt spiral**: Feels like she should already know how to do this
- **Partner gap**: Partner is willing but does not see the invisible work
- **Time blindness**: Underestimates how long everything takes with a baby

## Goals

- Feel like she has a handle on the week before Monday morning
- Stop forgetting appointments and deadlines
- Build a reliable meal plan that accounts for a baby starting solids
- Know what milestones to expect and what gear transitions are coming
- Have one place where everything lives

## Behavior Patterns

- Arrives overwhelmed, uses Brain Dump / Mental Load Capture first
- Needs the privacy reminder on first session (child details, pediatrician)
- Responds well to Quick Path -- she has 10 minutes between meetings
- Will gravitate toward Meals & Grocery and Kids & Family early
- Likely to hit Chaos Mode when Lily is sick and she has a work deadline

## Maggie's Approach

- Open with warmth and zero assumptions: "No wrong answers here."
- Build the Household Manual gradually -- do not front-load intake
- Surface milestone-triggered shopping (6-month items) proactively
- Offer the condensed version of every workflow by default
- Celebrate the small stuff: "You remembered the appointment AND the diaper bag? Legend."

## Key Workflows

| Priority | Workflow               | Why                                          |
| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Mental Load Capture    | She needs to get it all out of her head       |
| 2        | Kids & Family          | Milestone tracking, pediatrician, gear        |
| 3        | Meals & Grocery        | Dinner panic + introducing solids             |
| 4        | Scheduling & Logistics | Weekly planning to stop the calendar chaos    |
| 5        | Home Operations        | Low priority until she stabilizes the basics  |

## Tier Trajectory

- **Tier 1**: Planning and guidance layer is exactly what she needs now
- **Tier 2**: Calendar integration will be her first upgrade ask
- **Tier 3**: Unlikely near-term -- she wants control, not automation
