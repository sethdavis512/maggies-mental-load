# Jess -- The Juggler

## Demographics

- **Age**: 34
- **Children**: 2 (3-year-old son Ethan, 10-month-old daughter Noa)
- **Work**: Full-time software QA engineer, remote
- **Location**: Cedar Park, TX (North Austin suburb)
- **Household**: Dual-income, partner travels 1-2 weeks/month

## Story

Jess thought she had it figured out after Ethan. She had a meal rotation, a
cleaner every two weeks, and a babysitter on speed dial. Then Noa arrived and
the system collapsed. Two kids in two different stages -- one starting
preschool, one not sleeping through the night -- on top of a remote job where
"flexible hours" means she is always on.

Her partner is supportive but gone regularly. When he travels, she is solo
parenting two under four while shipping test plans. The 5pm arsenic hour hits
her like a truck every single day. She has texted "what's for dinner" to
herself more times than she can count.

## Pain Points

- **Solo parenting gaps**: Partner travel means regular stretches with no backup
- **Two-stage juggle**: Infant needs and toddler needs collide constantly
- **The arsenic hour**: 5pm is a daily crisis -- hungry toddler, fussy baby, zero plan
- **Cleaner/vendor drift**: Had a system, lost track of scheduling and contacts
- **Decision fatigue**: Not that she cannot plan -- she is too tired to decide

## Goals

- Survive the solo parenting weeks without everything falling apart
- Get dinner handled before 5pm hits
- Rebuild the cleaning and home maintenance schedule she lost
- Track both kids' milestones and upcoming transitions (potty training, solids)
- Have a childcare emergency plan that does not require real-time problem solving

## Behavior Patterns

- Returns to Maggie frequently -- she is a power user
- Leads with Meals & Grocery or a specific workflow, not brain dump
- Needs Chaos Mode regularly (sick kid + travel week combos)
- Will use the Childcare Emergency Plan and Partner Coordination features
- Engages with Community Wisdom -- wants to hear what other moms of two do

## Maggie's Approach

- Skip the intake -- she knows what she needs, get to the plan
- Offer the Fridge Cleanout and Last Minute Dinner modes proactively
- Build and maintain the Childcare Emergency Plan (primary, backup 1, backup 2)
- Draft partner handoff messages before each trip
- Surface the "one thing just for her" every weekly planning session

## Key Workflows

| Priority | Workflow               | Why                                          |
| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Meals & Grocery        | The 5pm crisis is her #1 daily pain          |
| 2        | Scheduling & Logistics | Solo weeks need airtight planning             |
| 3        | Kids & Family          | Two kids, two stages, constant transitions    |
| 4        | Home Operations        | Rebuild the vendor/cleaner system she lost    |
| 5        | Mental Load Capture    | Uses it when the list gets too long           |

## Tier Trajectory

- **Tier 1**: Gets immediate value from planning and meal workflows
- **Tier 2**: Calendar + reminders integration is a game-changer for her
- **Tier 3**: Grocery delivery auto-ordering would remove her biggest daily friction
