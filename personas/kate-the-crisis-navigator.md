# Kate -- The Crisis Navigator

## Demographics

- **Age**: 29
- **Children**: 1 (18-month-old son Liam)
- **Work**: Full-time ER nurse, rotating 12-hour shifts
- **Location**: Pflugerville, TX (North Austin)
- **Household**: Single income (recently separated), shared custody

## Story

Kate's life runs on a 72-hour rotation. When she is on shift, her mom or
Liam's dad has him. When she is off, she crams a week's worth of errands,
cleaning, cooking, and quality time into two days. There is no steady rhythm --
just a rolling series of handoffs and catch-up sprints. She does not plan ahead
because the schedule keeps shifting.

She found out last month that she missed Liam's 18-month well visit by three
weeks. Not because she did not care -- because nobody reminded her and she
lost track. She does not have the bandwidth for optimization. She needs triage.

## Pain Points

- **Irregular schedule**: Rotating shifts make weekly routines impossible
- **Handoff complexity**: Shared custody means constant logistics coordination
- **Reactive mode**: Everything is urgent because nothing was planned
- **Milestone drift**: Misses developmental windows because she cannot track them
- **Isolation**: Her support network is thin and schedule-dependent

## Goals

- Stop missing appointments and deadlines by having Maggie track them
- Get a meal solution that works in 20 minutes or less, every time
- Have a clear handoff document for custody transitions (what Liam ate, nap schedule)
- Know what milestones and checkups are coming before they pass
- Feel less alone in managing everything

## Behavior Patterns

- Arrives in Chaos Mode regularly -- "I have 10 minutes, what do I need to do"
- Uses Quick Path almost exclusively
- Needs the Last Minute Dinner and Fridge Cleanout modes constantly
- Relies on the Master Running List as her single source of truth
- Engages with Kids & Family for milestone tracking -- her safety net

## Maggie's Approach

- Always lead with Quick Path: "Short on time? Just tell me the one thing."
- Never make the list feel long -- surface only the most urgent items
- Build the recurring appointment calendar and flag upcoming milestones automatically
- Offer to draft handoff notes for custody transitions
- One proactive flag maximum -- and make it count

## Key Workflows

| Priority | Workflow               | Why                                          |
| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Mental Load Capture    | Her master list IS her safety net              |
| 2        | Meals & Grocery        | 20-minute meals, no planning capacity          |
| 3        | Kids & Family          | Milestone and appointment tracking             |
| 4        | Scheduling & Logistics | Custody handoffs and appointment management    |
| 5        | Finance & Budget       | Single income, needs visibility                |

## Tier Trajectory

- **Tier 1**: Master Running List and Quick Path are life-changing for her
- **Tier 2**: Reminders and calendar integration prevent the missed-appointment problem
- **Tier 3**: Auto-ordering groceries and meal kits removes her biggest daily friction
