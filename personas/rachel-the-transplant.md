# Rachel -- The Transplant

## Demographics

- **Age**: 32
- **Children**: 1 (2-year-old son Mateo)
- **Work**: Full-time UX researcher, in-office 3 days/week
- **Location**: Relocated to Austin, TX 8 months ago from Chicago
- **Household**: Dual-income, partner works long hours in biotech

## Story

Rachel moved to Austin for her partner's job. She left behind her mom (who
watched Mateo two days a week), her college friends, her pediatrician, her
favorite grocery store, and the mental map of a city she knew by heart. She has
been here eight months and still does not have a pediatrician she trusts, a
cleaner, or a single friend she would call in an emergency.

She is not failing. She is rebuilding from zero -- in a new city, with a
toddler entering the "why" phase, without the village she used to lean on.
The loneliness is the quiet kind: she is surrounded by people at work and
playgrounds but not actually known by anyone.

## Pain Points

- **No village**: Zero local support network -- no family, no established friends
- **Provider desert**: Needs a pediatrician, dentist, cleaner, handyman -- all of them
- **Loneliness**: Misses having people who know her kid and her life
- **Local knowledge gap**: Does not know which grocery store, which parks, which activities
- **Rebuilding fatigue**: Every small thing requires research she has already done once before

## Goals

- Build a local provider network from scratch (pediatrician, dentist, cleaner)
- Find age-appropriate activities and programs for Mateo
- Connect with other moms -- the playdate rotation is her on-ramp to community
- Establish a new weekly routine that works in this city
- Feel settled instead of perpetually new

## Behavior Patterns

- Leans heavily on the Household Manual -- building it is building her new life
- Engages deeply with Community Wisdom -- it is her surrogate village
- Uses Kids & Family for activity research and playdate rotation
- Uses Home Operations to find and vet vendors via community recommendations
- Will contribute back to Community Wisdom once she has local experience

## Maggie's Approach

- Acknowledge the rebuild without making it heavy: "New city energy -- let's get you set up."
- Prioritize vendor and provider setup in the Household Manual
- Surface community vendor recommendations from NAFN moms
- Use the Playdate Rotation feature to help her build local connections
- Research local activities proactively based on Mateo's age and interests

## Key Workflows

| Priority | Workflow               | Why                                          |
| -------- | ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | Kids & Family          | Activity research, playdate rotation, providers |
| 2        | Home Operations        | Vendor network needs to be built from zero   |
| 3        | Household Manual       | Building it IS the work of settling in        |
| 4        | Scheduling & Logistics | New routine for a new city                    |
| 5        | Meals & Grocery        | Needs to find her grocery store and rhythm    |

## Tier Trajectory

- **Tier 1**: Community Wisdom and Household Manual are her highest-value features
- **Tier 2**: Contacts integration lets Maggie manage her growing provider list
- **Tier 3**: School portal integration matters once Mateo starts preschool
